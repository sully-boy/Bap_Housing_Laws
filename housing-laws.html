California Housing Laws
Explained

Eight state laws reshaping what's possible on single-family and multifamily parcels across California. Scroll to explore each one.

01

Existing single-family lot

After urban lot split: two duplexes, four units

State Law

SB 9

Requires ministerial approval of single-family homes and duplexes and/or urban lot splits on single-family zoned parcels. Allows up to four residences per R-1 lot (i.e., two duplexes on two lots). SB 450 (2024) removed additional barriers, but there's more to do.
02

Attached conversion ADU + detached new construction ADU on a single-family lot

State Law

ADU Law

Local agencies must adopt an ADU ordinance meeting certain criteria and approve ADUs meeting certain state standards:
  • 1 attached/conversion ADU and 1 Junior ADU (<500 sf)
  • 1 detached new construction ADU
03

Qualifying vacant R-1 parcel

After ministerial subdivision: starter homes with ADUs

State Law

Starter Home Revitalization Act
Single-Family

Local agencies must ministerially approve residential subdivisions of up to 10 parcels on vacant single-family zoned parcels up to 1.5 acres meeting certain criteria.
  • Resulting parcels must be >1,200 sf in single-family zones
  • Average unit size <1,750 net habitable sf
  • Resulting lots create relatively low-cost ownership opportunity
04

State Law

SB 4
Faith Lands Act

Requires ministerial approval of residential projects meeting certain infill criteria on land owned by either an independent higher education or religious institution.
  • Projects must be 100% affordable to low income households (with up to 20% of units for moderate income)
  • Also allows childcare facilities
05

Existing multifamily site with surface parking

Detached ADUs added to underutilized surface parking

State Law

ADUs — Multi-Family
SB 1211

Development can match the number of existing units with up to 8 detached ADUs, and conversion of non-livable space for up to 25% of the existing units to create a new interior ADU.
07

Existing site

1st and 2nd floor with bonus units and reduced setbacks

Site plan with open space and landscape

State Law

Density Bonus Law

Incentivizes the private development of deed-restricted affordable housing by offering:
  • Density bonus up to 138.75%
  • Incentives/concessions
  • Waivers or reductions in development standards
  • Reduced parking ratios
  • Applicable to 5+ unit projects
08

Existing commercial building

Local agencies must permit the conversion of existing commercial/industrial buildings to residential or mixed use where qualifying projects meet certain affordability requirements and labor standards.

  • Rental: 8% low-income + 5% extremely low income units, or 15% lower income units
  • Owner-occupied: 30% moderate income or 15% lower income units
  • Historic preservation standards apply to buildings older than 50 years
